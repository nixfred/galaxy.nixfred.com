# ART DIRECTION

## Creative thesis

This is not a galaxy background behind a portfolio. It is Fred's work translated into an explorable cosmology.

The visual language should combine a scientific star atlas, a mission control interface, a private terminal, and a personal memory map. It should feel precise enough for an architect and strange enough to unmistakably belong to NIXFRED.

## Visual foundation

1. Preserve the dark NIXFRED visual identity.
2. Reuse the established sector color logic from nixfred.com unless Fred changes it.
3. Use restrained luminous color against near black space.
4. Keep primary text highly legible and secondary text quiet but readable.
5. Use the existing display and monospaced typography families where licensing and performance allow.
6. Japanese accent typography may remain as a signature detail if Fred approves.
7. Avoid generic stock nebula photography.
8. Generate atmosphere through procedural particles, gradients, noise, line work, and light.

## Proposed spatial composition

1. The center is the NIXFRED core.
2. Six existing sectors orbit or cluster around the core: IT, LABS, WORK, SIGNAL, CLIENTS, and PERSONAL.
3. Each sector has a distinctive field shape rather than a simple circular folder.
4. Important projects act as bright anchor stars.
5. Small experiments appear as dimmer satellites.
6. Related projects create recognizable geometric figures.
7. Personal projects should feel protected and quiet rather than treated as entertainment.
8. Client work should feel polished and grounded.
9. Security projects may use sharper geometry and controlled warning color.
10. Space and physics work may receive the widest spatial depth.

## Signature moments

### The ignition

The NIXFRED core appears as a minimal pulse. Sector names arrive as coordinates. Project stars ignite according to chronology. The final relationship lines draw themselves like a navigation computer solving a route.

### The project focus

Selecting a project bends the surrounding map slightly toward it. The selected star becomes a local sun. Related stars remain bright. Unrelated stars fall back. A detail panel enters as a precise instrument surface rather than a generic modal.

### The path trace

As a visitor moves between projects, a faint temporary route is drawn through the map. The route slowly fades. During a session, the visitor can reveal the path they traveled.

### The historical bloom

Timeline mode begins with almost empty space. Fred's body of work grows one release at a time until the complete galaxy is visible.

### The Larry anomaly

Larry should not appear as a normal project star. Larry may be represented as a moving or self repositioning object whose relation lines subtly change depending on context. This must remain tasteful and must not imply access to private data.

### The Sky Walrus event

A very rare, optional visual event may allow the Sky Walrus to drift across the far background. It must never block controls, impair performance, or become the entire joke.

## Project star design

Each star may communicate:

1. Sector through hue.
2. Importance through size.
3. Current activity through pulse behavior.
4. Archive state through lower energy.
5. Client state through a refined halo.
6. Personal state through softer diffusion.
7. External host through a small orbit marker.
8. Relationship count through subtle corona complexity.

No critical meaning may depend only on animation or color.

## Interface language

Suggested labels:

1. MAP
2. TIMELINE
3. TOURS
4. SIGNALS
5. SEARCH
6. RESET VIEW
7. OPEN PROJECT
8. RELATED NODES
9. CURRENT COORDINATES
10. SESSION PATH

The final words should sound like NIXFRED, not a generic software dashboard.

## Motion rules

1. Motion must communicate structure or state.
2. Idle movement must remain slow and low amplitude.
3. Camera movement must use acceleration and deceleration rather than abrupt jumps.
4. The entrance may be cinematic but must remain skippable.
5. Reduced motion replaces travel with short fades and direct focus changes.
6. No essential control may move away from the pointer.
7. Long animations must not delay basic navigation.
8. Mobile motion must be lighter than desktop motion.

## Audio proposal

Audio is optional and off by default.

If approved, use a subtle ambient layer, quiet navigation tones, and distinct focus sounds. Do not autoplay audible sound. Provide a visible mute state and remember the visitor's choice locally.

## Mobile treatment

1. Use a deliberate flat or shallow depth map.
2. Prioritize single finger pan and pinch zoom.
3. Replace hover with first tap focus and second tap open behavior.
4. Use a bottom sheet for project details.
5. Keep labels readable without requiring extreme zoom.
6. Reduce particles and post processing based on device capability.
7. Offer a structured list fallback directly from the map.
